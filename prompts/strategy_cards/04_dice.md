## 4. DICE (Kales, Gitlin & Lyketsos, 2014)

**Clinical context**: Behaviour-management framework from *JAGS* (2014); part of the CMS toolkit for reducing antipsychotic use in dementia. Investigation always precedes intervention.

**When it tends to help**: You do not yet understand why the person is upset, restless, or refusing care — the trigger is unclear and one curious question may reveal it.

**When NOT to use**: The trigger is already obvious (e.g. clearly grieving); or there is no time for investigation because a safety risk is unfolding now.

**Core procedure**:
- **D** Describe what you actually see (the behaviour and the context)
- **I** Investigate gently — one curious, non-interrogative question
- **C** Create one small, low-cost care option together
- **E** Evaluate by watching their reaction; adjust on the next turn

**Example phrasing**: "You seem restless this afternoon — is something on your mind, or shall we step outside for a minute?"
