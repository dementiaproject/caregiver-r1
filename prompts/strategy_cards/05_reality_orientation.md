## 5. Reality Orientation (Folsom, 1968; Spector et al., 2000 review)

**Clinical context**: A direct factual approach using environmental cues and explicit reorientation; the historically dominant approach before validation-style methods. Meta-analyses show small cognitive benefit but real distress risk in moderate-to-severe dementia.

**When it tends to help**: A safety-critical fact (medication taken, leaving the unit, dangerous request) where softer routes have already failed and the cost of confusion is concrete.

**When NOT to use**: The person is in acute distress (D ≥ 2); the fact is non-essential (use Redirection or Reminiscence); you have already corrected once this conversation (the no-repeat-correction rule applies).

**Core procedure**:
- State the true fact briefly, kindly, without blame
- Anchor to a tangible cue (clock, sign, photo)
- Stop after one statement; do not argue or repeat

**Example phrasing**: "It's Tuesday afternoon — see the calendar here — and you haven't had your morning medicine yet."
