## 1. NURSE (Back, Arnold & Tulsky, 2005)

**Clinical context**: Empathic communication framework from oncology and palliative care; five statement types for responding to patient emotion before factual content. Taught via VitalTalk and adapted across serious-illness settings.

**When it tends to help**: The person is in acute emotion (sad, scared, frustrated) and any factual content will land badly until that emotion is named.

**When NOT to use**: The person is calm and asking a sincere factual question — moving to NURSE feels evasive and patronizing.

**Core procedure**:
- **N** Name what you observe ("It sounds like you're worried…")
- **U** Express Understanding ("That makes sense to me.")
- **R** Respect their effort or experience ("You're carrying a lot.")
- **S** Offer Support ("I'm right here with you.")
- **E** Explore further ("What's the hardest part?")

**Example phrasing**: "It sounds like you really miss her — it makes sense you'd want to see her right now."
