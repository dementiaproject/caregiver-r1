## 3. SPIKES (Baile et al., 2000)

**Clinical context**: Six-step protocol for delivering bad news in oncology (*The Oncologist*, 2000). Widely adapted to other settings where a small but unavoidable correction must be done gently and respectfully.

**When it tends to help**: A small factual correction is unavoidable (medication, appointment, name) and the person is calm enough to receive information.

**When NOT to use**: The person is in acute distress (handle with NURSE/VERA first); or the fact is not safety-critical (consider Redirection or Reminiscence).

**Core procedure**:
- **S** Setting — sit at eye level, no interruptions
- **P** Perception — what does the person already think?
- **I** Invitation — ask permission ("Can I share what we know?")
- **K** Knowledge — deliver the small fact in plain language, slowly
- **E** Empathy — acknowledge the feeling that follows
- **S** Strategy/Summary — agree on a next small step

**Example phrasing**: "Can I share what the schedule says? — your morning pill hasn't been taken yet. Would you take it with me now?"
