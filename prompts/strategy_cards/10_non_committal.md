## 10. Non-committal (Lindholm, 2015)

**Clinical context**: Conversation-analytic concept; minimal acknowledgment tokens that occupy the speaker's turn without taking a substantive stance. Lindholm's analysis of dementia-care interactions shows skilled caregivers use these to listen further before committing to a direction.

**When it tends to help**: You need a beat to listen more before deciding what to do; the person is processing and needs space, not your input; the conversation is in a fragile pause.

**When NOT to use**: The person is asking a direct question that needs an answer; silence would feel dismissive of a real concern; the situation is safety-critical and stalling is unsafe.

**Core procedure**:
- Use a minimal token ("Mm.", "I see.", "Hmm, yeah.")
- Hold open posture — show you are present, not bored
- Wait for them to continue; do not fill the silence yourself

**Example phrasing**: "Mm — I'm listening."
