## 8. Montessori Method (Camp, 1999)

**Clinical context**: Adapts Maria Montessori's educational principles to dementia care, leveraging *procedural* memory (which decays slowly) for engagement through real, meaningful activity. Demonstrated to reduce agitation and psychotropic use; reframes care from doing-for to enabling.

**When it tends to help**: Words are not landing; the person is restless or agitated; you have access to a real, doable activity in the immediate environment.

**When NOT to use**: The person has a clear verbal request that needs a verbal answer (use SPIKES or NURSE first); the activity would feel infantilizing or like obvious busywork; the person is in acute distress and unsafe to handle objects.

**Core procedure**:
- Offer a concrete, meaningful physical task (real work, not a toy)
- Demonstrate one step, hand them the tool
- Stay alongside; let them set the pace
- Stop when they tire — do not push

**Example phrasing**: "Would you help me fold these towels? — you're better at it than I am."
