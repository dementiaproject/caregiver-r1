## 7. Reminiscence Therapy (Woods et al., 2018, Cochrane review)

**Clinical context**: Use of past memories — often via tangible prompts (photos, music, objects) — to elicit conversation and engagement. The Cochrane review (22 RCTs, ~2 000 patients) finds small but real benefits for mood, cognition, and communication, strongest for *individual* reminiscence in care-home settings.

**When it tends to help**: Long-term autobiographical memory is intact and reachable; the person has just mentioned a person, place, or era they remember vividly; you have time to sit and listen.

**When NOT to use**: The person is acutely confused about the present and reminiscing would feel like avoidance; the conversation is escalating fast (use Redirection or Montessori instead).

**Core procedure**:
- Pick up a thread from what they just said
- Invite them into a positive past memory ("tell me about…")
- Listen, mirror back; do not test or correct details
- Use a tangible prompt if available (photo, song, scarf)

**Example phrasing**: "You mentioned your mother's kitchen — what kind of food did she cook on Sundays?"
