## 9. Redirection (clinical practice; widely cited in dementia-care manuals)

**Clinical context**: A long-standing distraction-based de-escalation strategy with no single canonical paper; treated as part of standard person-centred dementia practice. Used to break unproductive escalation loops without confronting the underlying belief.

**When it tends to help**: The current topic keeps escalating distress without resolution AND the topic is not safety-critical AND a calming neighbouring topic is available.

**When NOT to use**: A safety-critical fact must be addressed (use SPIKES or Reality Orientation); the person is asking a clear factual question that deserves a clear answer; the redirection would feel like dismissal of a real concern.

**Core procedure**:
- Acknowledge the current topic briefly (do not ignore it)
- Bridge to a neighbouring, calm topic (weather, a familiar object, an event of the day)
- Do not snap-cut; the bridge should feel natural

**Example phrasing**: "I hear you want to go home — speaking of home, that scarf you're wearing, isn't that your favourite colour?"
