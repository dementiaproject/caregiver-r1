## 2. VERA (Blackhall, Hawkes et al., 2011)

**Clinical context**: A dementia-specific four-step framework developed for UK nursing education (Nursing Standard, 2011). Pairs validation of feeling with gentle redirection toward a calming activity.

**When it tends to help**: The person holds a confused belief that is emotionally important to them and the belief is not safety-critical.

**When NOT to use**: A real safety issue requires factual correction; or the person is asking a sincere factual question and validation would feel evasive.

**Core procedure**:
- **V** Validate the feeling, not the literal claim
- **E** Name the Emotion ("you sound worried about her")
- **R** Reassure — they are safe here, with you
- **A** Shift gently to a calming Activity

**Example phrasing**: "You miss her so much — let's sit by the window and you can tell me about her while we have some tea."
